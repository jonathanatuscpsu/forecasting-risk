Step 2: Model Identification

  • We use the Augmented Dickey-Fuller test (ADF) to determine its stationarity.

  • The null hypothesis for the test is that the data is non-stationary.

  • A p-value of ADF (0.3516) is greater than 0.05. Hence, we fail to reject the null hypothesis for this test.

library(tseries) # The `adf.test()` from the `tseries` package will do a Augmented Dickey-Fuller test 
adf.test(data_ts)
## 
##  Augmented Dickey-Fuller Test
## 
## data:  data_ts
## Dickey-Fuller = -2.5442, Lag order = 4, p-value = 0.3516
## alternative hypothesis: stationary

Step 2: Model Identification (cont’d)

  • Taking the difference

    • We take a first order difference of the time series data and check it with ADF again as follows.
    • A p-value of ADF (0.01) is less than 0.05. Therefore, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the data is stationary.

Step 4: Diagnostic Check (cont’d)

  • We use Ljung-Box test:
Box.test(resid(fit3), lag = 10, type = "Ljung-Box")
## 
##  Box-Ljung test
## 
## data:  resid(fit3)
## X-squared = 3.459, df = 10, p-value = 0.9685

which fails to reject the null hypothesis.

  • The results are statistically significant (i.e., the p-value<0.05). We fail to reject the null hypothesis \(H_{0}\). Thus, we can conclude that the residuals are NOT distinguishable from a white noise series.

  • Note: Ljung-Box test

    • In general, the Ljung-Box test (a test of autocorrelation of the residuals) is defined as:
      • \(H_{0}\) (null hypothesis): the autocorrelations come from a white noise series.
      • \(H_{a}\) (alternative hypothesis): the autocorrelations do not come from a white noise series.

  • Hence, our final model is the ARIMA(2,1,1):\((1-B)y_t=0.50y_{t-1}+0.34y_{t-2}\)-\(\varepsilon_{t-1}\)+\(\varepsilon_{t}\), where \(\varepsilon_t\sim(0, \sigma^2)\).
  • Note:
    • Autoregressive Moving Average models (ARMA):
      • \(y_{t}=c+\phi_{1}y_{t-1}+\cdots+\phi_{p}y_{t-p}+\theta_{1}\varepsilon_{t-1}+\cdots+\theta_{q}\varepsilon_{t -q}+\varepsilon_{t}\), where \(\varepsilon_t\) is white noise.
    • Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average models (ARIMA):
      • Combine ARMA model with differencing.
      • \((1-B)^d y_t\) follows an ARMA model.
      • A first difference can be written as \(y'_{t} = y_{t} - y_{t-1} = y_t - By_{t} = (1 - B)y_{t}\).
